Community Story Table – Bridging the Gap Between College Students and Boston's Chinatown
The Community Story Table was born from my experiences as a Chinese international student and conversations with peers who shared a sense of disconnect from Boston's Chinatown. Despite the physical proximity of Emerson College and other institutions to Chinatown, many students feel like outsiders in a community that should feel like home. This project seeks to address the gap in engagement, especially among young people aged 17 to 24, a demographic that now makes up a significant portion of Chinatown’s residents.
Project Details / Background
Research Insights:
Through interviews and research, several key findings emerged:
  • While Chinatown has initiatives to engage youth, there is a lack of targeted efforts for college students.
  • Many students struggle to connect with the community due to limited knowledge of Chinatown’s history and culture.
  • Although Chinatown is rich with cultural stories and historical resources, these are often not accessible or visible to newcomers.
  • Local restaurants and businesses serve as primary interaction points but often do not foster lasting connections.
The Community Story Table
To address these challenges, I developed the Community Story Table, an intervention set up in local Chinatown restaurants. The concept operates on two levels:
Physical Table The physical setup consists of a dining table in a Chinatown restaurant, displaying printed materials like historical images and brief descriptions of the neighborhood’s history. Each table features a QR code that leads visitors to the digital counterpart of the Community Story Table, encouraging diners to explore more in-depth stories.
Digital Table The digital version offers links to stories about Chinatown’s history and connections to local organizations and events, helping visitors stay informed about current community activities. A digital whiteboard, accessible via Miro, allows users to leave messages, share their reflections, or even add their own stories, creating a sense of belonging and contribution to Chinatown’s evolving narrative.
